Leadership and Organization: Section Two

People, Power & Change

The first question an organizer asks is not “what is my issue” but “who are my people”

  1. Identify the people who you hope to organize Not necessarily what I agree with. But when I think of leadership, I am biased towards leading a business team that are motivated by entrepreneurial goal and personal incentives. In such case, the problem should be defined first. However, in general sense, I agree that knowing for whom to fight/lead should be the priority.
  2. What are the resources, challenges, values of my people?
  3. How to enable them to turn the resources they have into the power they need.

A leader needs to map the power to begin finding answers to these questions.

One change that you want to see happening? -> Affordable and quality education for every child at least up to highschool level.

  1. Head(Vision): Digital education for free in YouTube

  2. Heart(courage): Courage to take on current education system, ability to change parent’s mind, pleading for funds.

  3. Hands(skills): Learn about what makes a quality child education, how to create an interactive remote learning.

  4. People: People frustrated by not having quality education. Poor kids that don’t have ability to get good education.

  5. Power: Changing mentality, changing system to move from rote learning.

  6. Change: Affordable and quality child education.

Coaching & Mentoring

Mentoring is telling people what to do. Coaching is asking people the right questions. Questions that break down the issues into smaller issues and talking about fixing/solving those smaller issues. Coaching is more about brainstroming the different possibilities, discussion regarding which to prioritize and basically giving them the opportunity to take decision for themselves instead of telling them what to do.

Different types of coaching:

  1. Strategic coaching
  2. Motivational coaching
  3. Skills-based coaching

What is Power? Power is the ability to achieve purpose - MLK

Lecture Notes:

One change I want to lead.

  1. What? Affordable child education
  2. How? Creating and sharing free content on the YouTube
  3. For whom? Children from lower class family
  4. By when? By December 2021

It’s important to identify a small group of people to whom you want to start working on your change.

As a leader it is important to keep purpose before your ego. Competition isn’t always bad. Since they have the same mission as you.

It is important to identify opposition - who opposes your campaign and holds the resources to fullfill your demand then you don’t have to change your strategy when someone tries to oppose you.

Exercise: Identify your competitors, supporters and opposition

  • Competitors: Third party resources offering quality education that maybe online or offline. Through websites or CDs
  • Supporters: People who advocate quality child education, people residing in foreign countries who feel their country lack good education
  • Opposition: private schools & government schools? Not sure how though… maybe by provocating child’s parents

Power with and Power over

Power is a good thing that a leader needs to do his work. Sometimes it is misused by leaders.

Power with

It has to do with finding common ground among different interests and building collective strength. ‘Power with’ can help build bridges across different interests to transform or reduce social conflict and promote equitable relations.
-> Competitors and supporters.

Power over

When people are denied access to important resources like land, healthcare, and jobs ‘power over’ perpetuates inequality, injustice and poverty.
->Opposition: Tuition Teachers

Power over might rule with weapons that are physical or by controlling the resources we need to live: money, food, medical care or by controlling more subtle resources: information, approval, love.
